IELTS Writing Task 2 Sample Answer: Agriculture (Cambridge IELTS 13)

Essay Prompt

In spite of the advances made in agriculture, many people around the world still go hungry. Why is this the case? What can be done about this problem?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
Despite remarkable advances in agricultural productivity, global hunger remains a persistent crisis affecting approximately 690 million people annually. This paradox stems not from insufficient production but from systemic poverty, conflict, and inequality; addressing it requires comprehensive solutions integrating economic development, governance reform, and equitable distribution mechanisms. The persistence of hunger reflects structural causes disconnected from agricultural capacity. Poverty represents the primary obstacle; food scarcity in many regions is simultaneous with food oversupply globally, yet impoverished populations lack purchasing power to access available nutrition. Armed conflict devastates agricultural infrastructure whilst displacing vulnerable populations; approximately one-third of world hunger occurs in conflict-affected regions. Environmental degradation—deforestation, soil depletion, water scarcity—undermines subsistence farming in developing nations despite global agricultural innovation. Critically, land concentration means agricultural profits accumulate amongst wealthy landowners whilst rural labourers remain impoverished. Sub-Saharan Africa exemplifies this paradox: the region produces substantial food yet experiences endemic malnutrition due to governance failures and poverty rather than production insufficiency. Comprehensive solutions must address these root causes. Poverty reduction through sustainable employment and education creates purchasing power essential for food access. International conflict resolution programmes restore agricultural stability and infrastructure; Rwanda's post-conflict reconstruction demonstrates how investing in farmer cooperatives and targeted food assistance measurably reduced malnutrition. Land reform redistributing holdings to smallholder farmers increases productivity whilst empowering rural communities. Climate-resilient agricultural techniques and irrigation infrastructure strengthen subsistence farming capacity in vulnerable regions. Additionally, strengthening supply chains and rural markets improves distribution efficiency. In conclusion, hunger persists despite agricultural abundance because poverty and inequality prevent food access, whilst conflict destabilises production regions. Technological advances alone cannot resolve hunger; integrated solutions spanning poverty reduction, conflict resolution, land reform, and infrastructure development are essential. Only through addressing underlying socioeconomic causes can agricultural advances translate into universal food security.

Word count: 290 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

Despite agricultural advances, global hunger persists due to poverty, conflict, and systemic inequality; addressing this requires integrated solutions spanning economic development, conflict resolution, and equitable distribution systems.

Body Paragraph 1

Agricultural advances have not translated to universal food security due to structural causes

  • Poverty prevents access despite food availability—hunger is economic, not agricultural
  • Armed conflict disrupts supply chains and displaces vulnerable populations
  • Wealth concentration means landowners profit whilst rural workers remain impoverished
  • Climate change and environmental degradation undermine local farming resilience

e.g. Sub-Saharan Africa produces substantial food yet experiences chronic malnutrition due to poverty and governance failures

Body Paragraph 2

Comprehensive solutions require addressing root causes through targeted interventions

  • Poverty reduction through education and employment creates purchasing power
  • International conflict resolution restores agricultural infrastructure and stability
  • Land reform and cooperative farming empower smallholder farmers
  • Invest in climate-resilient agriculture and irrigation in vulnerable regions

e.g. Rwanda's cooperative farming initiatives and targeted food assistance programmes reduced malnutrition measurably

Counterargument

Conclusion Strategy

Technology alone cannot solve hunger; integrated economic, political, and agricultural reforms are essential
