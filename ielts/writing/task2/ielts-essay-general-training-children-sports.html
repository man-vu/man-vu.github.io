IELTS Essay General Training: Children and Sports

Essay Prompt

Some people believe that children should do sports so that they will grow up as healthy adults, but others feel sports are just about enjoying yourself. Discuss these both views and give your own opinion.

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The question of children's sports participation encompasses two distinct yet complementary perspectives: health optimisation and recreational enjoyment. Rather than presenting binary opposition, these viewpoints represent interconnected dimensions of child development, both deserving integration in comprehensive sporting programmes. Health-focused advocates articulate compelling developmental rationales. Regular physical activity during childhood substantially reduces obesity prevalence and establishes cardiovascular health trajectories extending into adulthood. Beyond physiological benefits, sports participation cultivates essential character attributes: discipline, perseverance, teamwork, and resilience. Children engaging in structured athletic environments develop enhanced self-regulation, goal-setting capacities, and interpersonal competencies. Research demonstrates children participating in regular sports exhibit significantly superior health metrics and psychological outcomes compared to sedentary peers. Conversely, recreation-focused proponents emphasise intrinsic motivation through enjoyment. Paradoxically, emphasising health benefits without cultivating genuine pleasure frequently produces counterproductive outcomes; children coerced into uninspiring activities typically abandon physical engagement entirely upon reaching adulthood. Conversely, sports genuinely enjoyed during childhood establish lifelong exercise habits. Enjoyment reduces psychological pressure, anxiety, and sport-related burnout, whilst simultaneously facilitating authentic social development and confidence-building through voluntary participation. The most effective approach synthesises both perspectives. Rather than positioning health and enjoyment as opposing objectives, enlightened sporting programmes prioritise discovering diverse activities where children find intrinsic satisfaction while naturally accumulating health benefits. Adults should facilitate exploration across varied sports, encouraging persistent engagement with personally resonant pursuits. When children discover genuinely enjoyable physical activities, health development follows organically, establishing sustainable wellness habits extending throughout lifespans. Thus, the dichotomy between health development and recreational enjoyment proves false; optimal child development requires purposefully integrating both dimensions.

Word count: 253 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

Whilst both health development and enjoyment constitute valid rationales for children's sports participation, the most comprehensive approach integrates both perspectives, recognising that sustained physical engagement fundamentally requires intrinsic enjoyment.

Body Paragraph 1

Health-focused advocates emphasise sports' developmental benefits

  • Physical activity prevents obesity and cardiovascular disease
  • Sports develop discipline, teamwork, and resilience
  • Childhood fitness habits establish lifelong health trajectories

e.g. Children engaging in regular sports demonstrate 40% lower obesity rates and superior cardiovascular health into adulthood

Body Paragraph 2

Recreation-focused proponents prioritise enjoyment as primary motivation

  • Intrinsic enjoyment ensures sustained participation
  • Fun-focused approaches reduce psychological pressure and anxiety
  • Play develops social skills and confidence more effectively

e.g. Sports-averse children pressured into activities often abandon physical activity entirely, whereas enjoyment-motivated participation continues into adulthood

Counterargument

While health benefits appear more measurable, research demonstrates enjoyment predicts long-term adherence better than health messaging alone

Conclusion Strategy

The optimal approach combines both: structured sports offering health benefits while emphasising enjoyment, exploring diverse activities until children discover personally resonant pursuits
