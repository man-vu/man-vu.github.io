Cambridge 16 IELTS Essay: The Best Time in History

Essay Prompt

Some people believe that now is the best time in history to be alive, while others argue that life was better in the past. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The question of whether contemporary life represents the optimal historical epoch merits nuanced examination. Whilst modern technology and medicine indisputably confer substantial advantages, evidence increasingly suggests that technological progress has not proportionally enhanced overall quality of life for most populations. Proponents of contemporary superiority marshal compelling arguments. Medical breakthroughs—antibiotics, vaccines, surgical innovations—have systematically eliminated previously fatal diseases and extended life expectancy dramatically. Digital communication networks have democratised information access, enabling instantaneous global collaboration and knowledge sharing unimaginable to previous generations. Furthermore, material living standards across developed nations have risen exponentially, with goods and services previously accessible only to elites now available to broader populations. Conversely, critics highlight overlooked dimensions of past life quality. Modern existence frequently involves elevated stress levels, employment precarity, and psychological anxiety despite material abundance. The same technologies promising connection often paradoxically intensify isolation and social fragmentation. Traditional communities exhibited stronger social cohesion, more meaningful interpersonal relationships, and greater work-life balance despite material scarcity. Contemporary people sacrifice leisure time, community engagement, and existential meaning for technological convenience. However, comparing epochs requires acknowledging trade-offs rather than romanticising the past. Premodern life involved genuine hardship, shortened lifespans, and limited opportunities. The optimal scenario synthesises both periods: preserving modern medical advancement and knowledge accessibility whilst cultivating community strength, reducing working hours, and ensuring technology serves human flourishing rather than dominating existence. Ultimately, we require deliberate restructuring of contemporary society to reclaim past social values whilst maintaining scientific progress.

Word count: 238 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

While modern life offers unprecedented technological and medical advantages, meaningful quality-of-life improvements for most people remain limited compared to perceptions of past simplicity and community.

Body Paragraph 1

Many argue contemporary life represents optimal historical conditions

  • Medical technology has eliminated previously fatal diseases
  • Digital communication enables global connection and knowledge access
  • Living standards and access to goods have increased significantly

e.g. Antibiotics have increased life expectancy by decades, and smartphones provide unprecedented information access

Body Paragraph 2

Others contend past life offered superior quality and meaning

  • Modern work stress and inequality create mental health crises
  • Digital connectivity paradoxically increases isolation and anxiety
  • Communities were stronger and relationships more meaningful in traditional societies

e.g. Medieval communities had stronger social bonds despite lower material wealth, and modern technology increases work pressure rather than reducing it

Counterargument

While nostalgia for the past ignores genuine hardships and reduced life expectancy, modern technology's benefits do not translate universally to improved wellbeing

Conclusion Strategy

The best time combines past and present: we need modern medical advances alongside stronger communities, reduced work hours, and technology serving rather than dominating our lives
