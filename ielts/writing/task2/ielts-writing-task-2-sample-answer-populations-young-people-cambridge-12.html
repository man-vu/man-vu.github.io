IELTS Writing Task 2 Sample Answer: Populations, Young People (Cambridge 12)

Essay Prompt

At the present time, the population of some countries includes a relatively large number of young adults, compared with the number of older people. Do the advantages of this situation outweigh the disadvantages?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
A youthful demographic structure presents a nuanced situation warranting careful analysis of both benefits and challenges. While certain disadvantages exist, the advantages of a predominantly young population substantially outweigh the drawbacks, particularly regarding economic productivity and societal dynamism. The economic benefits are compelling. A large cohort of working-age adults expands the labour force, driving productivity and economic growth; countries like India and Vietnam exemplify sustained expansion fuelled by youthful demographics. Lower dependency ratios—fewer elderly and children relative to working-age adults—reduce fiscal pressure on pension systems and healthcare infrastructure, freeing resources for productive investment. Furthermore, young populations are incubators of innovation; younger workers embrace technological change and entrepreneurship more readily, fostering competitive advantage in knowledge economies. Consumer spending patterns also differ; younger demographics typically exhibit higher consumption rates, stimulating economic activity and retail expansion across sectors. The disadvantages, while real, are substantially less severe than those facing ageing societies. Youth unemployment represents a legitimate concern if job creation fails to match labour supply, yet this issue remains manageable through targeted skills training and educational reform. Overcrowding in educational facilities and housing shortages constitute infrastructure challenges requiring investment, but they reflect growth rather than economic decline. Ageing societies, conversely, confront irreversible labour force shrinkage, unsustainable welfare systems, and demographic collapse. In conclusion, while policy makers must address youth unemployment through strategic job creation and education quality, a youthful population remains a substantial demographic dividend. Countries with predominantly young populations possess intrinsic advantages in economic growth, innovation capacity, and fiscal sustainability compared to ageing counterparts. Therefore, the advantages demonstrably outweigh the disadvantages, provided governments implement appropriate investments in employment and skills development.

Word count: 269 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

Although a youthful population structure presents certain economic challenges, the advantages—including greater workforce productivity and innovation—substantially outweigh the drawbacks.

Body Paragraph 1

A young population offers significant economic and social advantages

  • Large workforce provides labour supply and economic growth
  • Lower dependency ratios reduce pressure on healthcare and pensions
  • Youth drive innovation and technological advancement
  • Increased consumer spending stimulates economic activity

e.g. Countries like India and Nigeria with young populations experience sustained economic growth and expansion in tech sectors

Body Paragraph 2

Disadvantages exist but are manageable compared to ageing societies

  • Unemployment and underemployment if job creation lags
  • Educational infrastructure strain and resource demands
  • Housing shortage and pressure on urban services

e.g. Youth unemployment in some Mediterranean nations; however, targeted investment in education and job training address these issues

Counterargument

Conclusion Strategy

Acknowledge challenges but emphasise that a youthful demographic is an asset when managed through appropriate policy investment in education and employment
