The Most Important Aim of Science

Essay Prompt

The most important aim of science ought to be to improve people's lives. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The proposition that science's paramount objective should constitute improving human welfare merits substantial endorsement, though this should not preclude foundational research generating knowledge essential for genuine long-term progress. A balanced perspective acknowledges science's ethical imperative whilst recognizing that basic research ultimately enables transformative applications. Indisputably, science's humanitarian obligation represents its most profound responsibility. Medical science exemplifies this principle: vaccine development dramatically reduced mortality from infectious diseases, extending billions of lives. Agricultural innovations addressing food security, clean water technologies providing sanitation, and public health measures preventing disease transmission collectively demonstrate science's capacity to alleviate human suffering at scale. These achievements justify substantial research investment directed explicitly toward solving pressing human problems. Conversely, restricting science exclusively to immediate human-welfare applications would paradoxically undermine science's ultimate humanitarian potential. Theoretical physics appeared abstract and impractical; subsequently generated understanding enabling quantum mechanics, semiconductors, and digital technologies transforming modern existence. Similarly, basic biology research investigating cellular mechanisms creates foundational knowledge enabling medical breakthroughs impossible without antecedent understanding. History reveals numerous instances where seemingly irrelevant theoretical discoveries eventually yielded unexpected practical applications. Optimal scientific advancement integrates both dimensions: prioritizing human-welfare-focused research addressing immediate needs whilst simultaneously supporting foundational investigation generating knowledge for future innovation. This balanced approach recognises that pure research represents investment rather than indulgence. Therefore, whilst agreeing that improving people's lives constitutes science's most important aim, acknowledging theoretical research's essential role provides more sophisticated understanding of how science ultimately serves humanity.

Word count: 236 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

While improving human welfare constitutes science's paramount ethical obligation, restricting science exclusively to utilitarian aims would eliminate foundational knowledge development and technological innovation ultimately essential for genuine progress.

Body Paragraph 1

Improving human lives represents science's crucial ethical imperative and demonstrates science's inherent value

  • Medical breakthroughs extending lifespan and reducing suffering derive from human-welfare-focused research
  • Agricultural science innovations address food security benefiting billions
  • Public health measures prevent disease and improve quality of life

e.g. Vaccine development dramatically reduced mortality from infectious diseases, exemplifying science's humanitarian purpose

Body Paragraph 2

Conversely, pure research generating foundational knowledge enables future practical applications and human advancement

  • Theoretical physics discoveries establish principles underlying technologies created decades later
  • Basic biology research generates understanding enabling medical innovations
  • Fundamental knowledge often yields unexpected practical applications

e.g. Quantum mechanics seemed purely theoretical; subsequently enabled semiconductor technology revolutionizing modern society

Counterargument

Excessive focus on basic research without practical applications risks irrelevance and misallocation of resources

Conclusion Strategy

Science should prioritize human welfare whilst maintaining foundational research; optimal approach integrates both dimensions
