Advertising New Products and Marketing Strategy

Essay Prompt

In their advertising, businesses nowadays usually emphasise that their products are new in some way. Why is this? Do you think it is a positive or negative development?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
While the emphasis on product novelty reflects genuine competitive dynamics and consumer psychology, I contend this marketing orientation presents predominantly negative societal consequences, promoting wasteful consumption, planned obsolescence, and diminished environmental sustainability. The trend reflects calculated commercial strategy with troubling implications. Businesses emphasize novelty for rational economic reasons. Psychological research demonstrates that consumers preferentially attract to innovation narratives and progress associations. Novelty claims effectively differentiate otherwise commodified markets, justifying premium pricing in saturated categories. Consumer upgrading cycles generate recurring revenue essential for sustained profitability. Smartphone manufacturers, for instance, emphasize incremental innovations—marginally improved processors, cameras, battery capacity—despite minimal functional improvements, deliberately encouraging annual replacement cycles. Additionally, genuine technological advancement sometimes produces authentic capability improvements warranting marketing emphasis. Conversely, this strategy generates substantial societal disadvantages. The novelty emphasis accelerates planned obsolescence cycles, encouraging consumers to discard functional products prematurely. Psychological pressure to maintain products at technological frontiers promotes unsustainable consumption patterns contradicting environmental imperatives. Manufacturers deprioritize durability and repairability in product design, deliberately engineering shorter product lifecycles. Consequently, electronic waste accumulation reaches catastrophic proportions—approximately 50 million tons annually—depleting rare mineral resources and exposing communities to toxic materials. Environmental costs of manufacturing and disposal substantially exceed benefits from marginal technological improvements. In conclusion, while business emphasis on novelty reflects understandable profit maximization strategies, the societal costs substantially outweigh benefits. Sustainable futures require reorienting marketing toward durability, longevity, and environmental responsibility rather than perpetual upgrade cycles. Regulatory intervention mandating repairability standards and extended producer responsibility could fundamentally redirect this counterproductive trend.

Thesis

While the emphasis on novelty reflects genuine competitive dynamics and consumer preferences, I contend this trend presents predominantly negative consequences, promoting wasteful consumption, premature obsolescence, and diminished product durability.

Body Paragraph 1

Multiple commercial and psychological factors explain businesses' emphasis on product novelty.

  • Novelty appeals to psychological preference for innovation and progress
  • New product claims differentiate commodified markets and justify premium pricing
  • Consumer upgrading cycles generate recurring revenue and sustained profitability
  • Technological advancement genuinely produces new capabilities and efficiencies

e.g. Smartphone manufacturers emphasize incremental innovations (marginally improved cameras, processors) to justify annual upgrades despite minimal functional improvements.

Body Paragraph 2

However, this orientation generates substantial societal disadvantages.

  • Novelty emphasis accelerates planned obsolescence and resource waste
  • Psychological pressure to purchase new products promotes unsustainable consumption patterns
  • Durability and repairability receive diminished priority in product design
  • Environmental costs of manufacturing and disposal escalate substantially

e.g. Electronics designed for programmed obsolescence create exponential e-waste accumulation, depleting rare minerals and increasing toxic exposure.

Counterargument

Some argue that novelty emphasis drives genuine technological progress benefiting society.

Conclusion Strategy

Acknowledge legitimacy of innovation while emphasizing need for sustainable consumption paradigms prioritizing durability and longevity over perpetual newness.
