Supermarkets and Global Food Production

Essay Prompt

In many countries nowadays, consumers can go to a supermarket and buy food produced all over the world. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The emergence of globally integrated food distribution networks through supermarkets presents a complex phenomenon offering consumer benefits whilst generating substantial environmental and social costs that ultimately render this development predominantly negative. Whilst acknowledging genuine advantages, evidence suggests disadvantages substantially outweigh positive outcomes. Global food networks undoubtedly provide consumer benefits. Supermarkets enable access to diverse foods regardless of local geography or season; northern European consumers purchase tropical fruits during winter months, enhancing nutritional diversity and dietary wellbeing. International agricultural trade creates employment in developing nations where farming constitutes primary economic sector. Competitive pricing resulting from global supply chains benefits economically disadvantaged consumers requiring affordable nutrition. Conversely, environmental consequences of global food distribution are catastrophic. International transportation generates enormous carbon emissions; strawberries shipped 8,000 miles produce carbon footprints exceeding locally-grown seasonal alternatives by several-hundred percent. Industrial-scale agriculture required to supply global networks causes soil degradation, aquifer depletion, and biodiversity loss. Furthermore, global food distribution systematically undermines local agricultural communities. Small-scale local farmers cannot economically compete with subsidised global producers, forcing abandonment of traditional farming practices and accelerating rural depopulation. This phenomenon particularly affects developing nations where agricultural displacement removes employment options. Additionally, globalised food systems create fragile supply chains vulnerable to disruption, as demonstrated during pandemic-related logistics failures. Locally-integrated food systems prove more resilient and sustainable. Therefore, whilst global food distribution offers superficial consumer convenience, environmental degradation, agricultural displacement, and systemic fragility substantially outweigh benefits. Optimal development prioritises rebuilding localised food systems that simultaneously support environmental sustainability, agricultural livelihoods, and community resilience.

Word count: 250 words·Target: 250+ words for Task 2

Thesis

Whilst global food distribution through supermarkets provides consumer choice and economic opportunities, the development is predominantly negative due to environmental degradation from transportation, agricultural exploitation, and undermining of local food systems.

Body Paragraph 1

Global food networks offer consumer benefits including dietary variety and year-round availability

  • Consumers access diverse foods regardless of local climate or season
  • International food trade creates agricultural employment in developing nations
  • Competitive pricing benefits low-income consumers requiring affordable nutrition

e.g. Northern European consumers can purchase fresh tropical fruits during winter months, enhancing dietary diversity

Body Paragraph 2

However, environmental and social costs substantially outweigh consumer benefits

  • Transportation emissions from international shipping generate massive carbon footprints
  • Industrial agriculture in source countries causes soil degradation and water depletion
  • Local farming becomes economically unviable, displacing traditional agricultural communities

e.g. Strawberries shipped 8,000 miles produce carbon equivalent exceeding local seasonal alternatives by hundreds of percent

Counterargument

Global food trade provides affordable nutrition for developing nations where local agriculture proves inadequate

Conclusion Strategy

Acknowledge trade benefits while emphasizing environmental sustainability priorities; advocate for localized food systems
