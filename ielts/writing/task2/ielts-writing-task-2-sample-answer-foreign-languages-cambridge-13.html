IELTS Writing Task 2 Sample Answer: Foreign Languages (Cambridge 13)

Essay Prompt

Living in a country where you have to speak a foreign language can cause serious social problems, as well as practical problems. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?

Band 9 Essay

Hover highlighted words for definitions
Language barriers unquestionably create communication obstacles for residents operating in non-native linguistic environments. However, characterising language difference as inevitably causing 'serious problems' oversimplifies complex contextual factors that substantially mediate these challenges. I partially disagree with the statement, arguing that problems are neither inevitable nor universally serious. Language barriers demonstrably create genuine difficulties. Employment outcomes deteriorate substantially for non-fluent speakers; wage gaps and occupational downgrading persist despite equivalent qualifications, reflecting employer discrimination and communication barriers. Healthcare access becomes compromised when language limitations prevent symptom articulation and treatment comprehension, creating serious safety vulnerabilities. Social integration proves difficult; linguistic barriers reduce community participation opportunities and increase isolation risks, particularly for long-term residents. Administrative navigation—housing, legal, financial matters—requires linguistic fluency; barriers create genuine practical disadvantages. However, empirical outcomes demonstrate that problems are not inevitable consequences of linguistic difference but rather depend substantially on systemic support infrastructure and host community receptiveness. Comprehensive language programmes significantly accelerate fluency acquisition, reducing employment disadvantages and integration timescales. Multicultural societies with established translation services, healthcare interpreters, and multilingual community members minimise practical obstacles. Psychologically, individuals with existing social networks and resilience exhibit reduced isolation despite language barriers. Furthermore, language learning provides cognitive benefits and personal agency; framing it solely as problem-creation neglects empowering dimensions. Canada's integration policies demonstrate that systematic language support and multicultural attitudes substantially mitigate barriers other nations experience. In conclusion, whilst language barriers create measurable initial obstacles, characterising them as inherently producing 'serious problems' overlooks mediating factors. Problems emerge not from linguistic difference itself but from inadequate systemic support and hostile host attitudes. Effective language programmes and inclusive communities demonstrate that barriers, whilst real, need not precipitate the serious consequences implied by the statement.

Thesis

Whilst language barriers create genuine communication obstacles, the extent of 'serious problems' depends substantially on contextual factors including migrant demographics, duration of residence, and host community attitudes rather than language difference itself.

Body Paragraph 1

Language barriers demonstrably create social and practical disadvantages for non-native speakers

  • Employment prospects diminish without fluency affecting career progression
  • Healthcare and administrative communication breakdowns create safety vulnerabilities
  • Social isolation emerges from communication difficulties limiting community integration
  • Educational access restricted for children with language gaps

e.g. Migrant workers frequently experience wage depression and occupational downgrading despite equivalent qualifications

Body Paragraph 2

However, problems are not inevitable; institutional support and community reception significantly mitigate language barriers

  • Comprehensive language programmes accelerate acquisition reducing integration timescales
  • Multicultural communities with translation services minimize practical obstacles
  • Psychological resilience and established social networks buffer isolation effects
  • Language learning itself provides cognitive benefits and personal growth

e.g. Canada's integration policies including public language support demonstrate successful multilingual settlement despite linguistic barriers

Counterargument

Language barriers are inevitable challenges, yet 'serious problems' depends on systemic support rather than linguistic difference alone

Conclusion Strategy

Language barriers present real obstacles, but characterising them as inherently 'serious' neglects systemic solutions and individual agency in language acquisition
