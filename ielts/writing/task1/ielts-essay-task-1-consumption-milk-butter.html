IELTS Essay Task 1: Consumption of Milk and Butter

Data Table

Task Prompt

The table below illustrates weekly consumption by age group of dairy products in a European country.

Band 9 Response

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The table illustrates the weekly consumption patterns of milk and butter across four distinct age groups within a European country. The data reveals marked variations in dairy product consumption, particularly with regard to milk, which demonstrates an inverse correlation with age progression. Milk consumption exhibits a pronounced downward trajectory as age increases. Children aged 5-11 constitute the highest consumers, with consumption figures significantly exceeding those of all other demographic groups. Adolescents aged 12-17 maintain relatively elevated consumption levels, though noticeably lower than their younger counterparts. The consumption pattern shifts considerably for working-age adults aged 18-59, which demonstrates a substantial decline compared to younger populations. The elderly population aged 60 and beyond exhibits the lowest milk consumption figures, reflecting a diminishing preference or physiological capacity for dairy intake with advancing age. In contrast, butter consumption remains comparatively stable across all age demographics, with only marginal fluctuations observed between groups. Notably, butter consumption figures are substantially lower than those recorded for milk across every age category. The elderly population demonstrates a relatively higher proportion of butter consumption compared to their milk intake, suggesting a gradual shift in dairy product preference with age progression. This inverse relationship between milk and butter consumption in older demographics suggests changing dietary patterns, potentially attributable to physiological factors such as lactose intolerance or altered nutritional requirements. Overall, the data underscores the significance of age as a determining factor in dairy product consumption patterns, with milk consumption being predominantly concentrated among younger populations.

Overview

The table presents weekly consumption figures for milk and butter across four age groups in a European nation, revealing distinct consumption patterns relative to age demographics.

Grouping Strategy

Organize by age progression while comparing product consumption patterns; highlight contrasts between age groups

Paragraph 1 Focus

Milk consumption patterns across age groups

  • Children aged 5-11 consume the highest milk consumption
  • Adolescents aged 12-17 show moderately high consumption
  • Working-age adults aged 18-59 demonstrate decreased consumption
  • Elderly population aged 60+ exhibits the lowest consumption figures

Paragraph 2 Focus

Butter consumption trends and comparison with milk

  • Butter consumption remains relatively consistent across age groups
  • Inverse relationship between milk and butter consumption in younger groups
  • Elderly population shows increased butter relative to milk
  • Overall butter consumption significantly lower than milk across all demographics
