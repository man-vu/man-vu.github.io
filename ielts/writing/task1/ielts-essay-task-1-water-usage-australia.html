IELTS Essay Task 1 Water Usage Australia

Chart / Graph

Task Prompt

The charts show the total usage proportions for water in Australia and a breakdown of its residential purposes.

Band 9 Response

Hover highlighted words for definitions
The two charts illustrate water consumption patterns in Australia, with the first depicting the proportional distribution across major economic sectors and the second providing detailed analysis of residential usage breakdown. Overall, Australia's water consumption is dominated by agricultural activities, while residential water use comprises a modest proportion of the nation's total water consumption despite its environmental significance. According to the first chart, agriculture accounts for the overwhelming majority of water usage in Australia, consuming approximately 70% of the nation's total water supply. This proportion is substantially more than the industrial sector, which utilizes roughly 19% of total water resources across manufacturing and processing facilities. Municipal water consumption, encompassing both residential and commercial applications, represents the smallest proportion at approximately 11% of total national usage. The significant disparity between agricultural and other sectors clearly reflects Australia's substantial farming operations, extensive livestock grazing requirements, and the climatic conditions that necessitate intensive irrigation systems across vast agricultural regions. The residential component, examined in greater detail in the second chart, reveals that domestic water use is concentrated in three primary consumption categories. Outdoor water consumption, primarily for garden irrigation and lawn maintenance, constitutes the largest residential category, accounting for approximately 42% of all household water use. This is followed by toilets, which consume roughly 28% of residential water consumption. Personal hygiene activities including bathing and showering account for approximately 19% of household water usage. Laundry operations and dishwashing combined constitute the remainder at approximately 11% of residential consumption. These proportions emphasize the substantial environmental impact of outdoor landscape maintenance in Australia's notably water-stressed regions. In conclusion, the data demonstrates that agricultural water usage dominates Australian consumption at 70%, whilst residential outdoor watering represents the most significant domestic water demand at 42% of household usage. This distribution underscores the necessity for sustainable water management strategies across both agricultural and residential sectors to ensure long-term water security.

Overview
